Big Bend National Park

Big Bend National Park Location
It is located in West Texas, in the Chihuahuan Desert, near Alpine, Texas.
Big Bend National Park Status
Big Bend Became a National Park on June 20, 1935.
Big Bend National Park Name
The Name Big Bend Comes From a Large Bend in the Rio Grande River Along the Park Boundary.
Big Bend National Park Area
Total Area: 801,163 acres
Big Bend Highest Elevation
The Highest Elevation found in Big Bend is 7,832ft on Emory Peak.
Big Bend Lowest Elevation
The Lowest Elevation found in Big Bend is 1,715ft at the Rio Grande River.
Big Bend National Park Wildlife
There are 56 species of reptiles, 75 species of mammals, more than 450 species of birds and at least 1,200 species of plants.
Big Bend Visitors
Number of Visitors in 2019 was 463,832.
Big Bend Park Activities
Activities include scenic drives, programs led by Big Bend Park Rangers, and stargazing.
Big Bend National Park Fee
The entrance fee for Big Bend National Park is $30 per vehicle per week.

Yellowstone National Park

  • 3,472 square miles.
  • Larger than Rhode Island and Delaware combined.
  • Home of Old Faithful Geyser.
  • Old Faithful erupts every 44 minutes to 2 hours.

Yosemite National Park

  • Famous for its giant Sequoia Trees.
  • Yosemite Valley was formed by glaciers.
  • Home to one of the tallest waterfalls in North America.
  • Yosemite's granite rock formations glow like fire at sunset.

Grand Canyon National Park

  • The canyon is full of hidden caves.
  • Only 335 caves have been recorded.
  • The Hopi Tribe considers the Grand Canyon a gateway to the afterlife.
  • The most dangerous animal in the canyon is the rock squirrel.
